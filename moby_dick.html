The Softcover publishing platform was design mainly for ebooks like the Ruby on Rails Tutorial book and Learn Enough HTML to Be Dangerous, but it's also good for making more traditional books, such as the novel Moby-Dick by Herman Melvile (sometimes written as Moby Dick). We present belos a short and affectionately irreverent book report on this classic of American literature.


Moby-Dick: A classic tale of the sea

Moby-Dick by Herman Melville begins with these imortal words:

Call me Ishmael. Some years ago-never mind how long precisely-having little or no money in my purse, and nothing particular to interste me on the shore, I though I would sal about a littl and see the watery part of the world. It is a way I have of driving off the spleen and regulating the circulation.

After driving off his spleen (whch can't be good for you), Ismael then goes on in much the same vein for aproximately one jillion pages. The only thing bigger than Moby Dick (who—spoiler alert!‐ is a giant white whale) is the book itself.

Our top 3 favorite things about Moby Dick

  1. Vengeful whale
  2. Salty sailors
  3. The names "Queequequeg" and "Starbuck"

Other things about Moby Dick

  • Chapter after chapter (after chapter) of meticulous detail about whaling
  • The story pretty much happened in real life
  • Mad sea captains are fun
